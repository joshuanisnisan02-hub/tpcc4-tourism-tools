Course Description

What is this course really about?

This course focuses on the application of business productivity tools, digital communication platforms, reservation and booking technologies, CRM concepts, online marketing tools, spreadsheet-based reporting, and data-driven decision-making in tourism settings.

💡 Key word: APPLICATION – You don't just learn about tools. You USE them to create real tourism business outputs.

Learning Outcomes

Course Learning Outcomes (1–4)

  • CLO 1: Explain the role of business tools and technologies in tourism operations
  • CLO 2: Use productivity, communication, collaboration, and cloud-based tools for tourism tasks
  • CLO 3: Apply basic concepts of reservation, booking, CRM, payment, and business information systems
  • CLO 4: Prepare digital documents, itineraries, tour costing, reports, presentations, and promotional materials
Learning Outcomes

Course Learning Outcomes (5–8)

  • CLO 5: Analyze simple tourism-related data using spreadsheets and visual reports
  • CLO 6: Demonstrate responsible and ethical use of digital tools and customer information
  • CLO 7: Develop a technology-based business output or workflow for a tourism need
  • CLO 8: Demonstrate collaboration, professionalism, creativity, and accuracy in tech-enabled tasks

Industry Context

Tourism today is digital-first

The tourism industry has undergone massive digital transformation. Travelers expect instant information, seamless online booking, digital payments, and real-time communication.

📱 Mobile

70%+ of travelers research trips on mobile devices

🌐 Online

Most bookings happen through OTAs and direct websites

💬 Instant

Customers expect real-time replies via chat and social media

Transformation

Manual vs. Digital Operations

❌ Before (Manual)

  • Handwritten reservation logbooks
  • Phone-only inquiries (missed calls = lost sales)
  • Paper-based itineraries & receipts
  • No customer history tracking
  • Word-of-mouth only marketing

✅ After (Digital)

  • Real-time online booking systems
  • 24/7 chat, email, social media inquiries
  • Digital docs, e-receipts, cloud backups
  • CRM with full customer profiles
  • Targeted digital marketing campaigns
Case Example

A local tour operator goes digital

Scenario: A small Cebu-based tour operator used to rely on Facebook Messenger inquiries, manual computation on paper, and cash-only payments. After adopting simple digital tools:

📄 Google Docs

Professional itineraries sent as PDF to customers

📊 Google Sheets

Automated costing with formulas, no more computation errors

💳 GCash/Maya

Digital payments, instant confirmation, fewer no-shows

Key Insight

Technology supports service quality

  • Speed: Faster responses, instant confirmations, real-time updates
  • Accuracy: Fewer errors in pricing, scheduling, and customer details
  • Consistency: Standardized documents, templates, and processes
  • Personalization: Customer preferences tracked for better service
  • Reach: Market to thousands through digital channels, not just walk-ins

Activity

Let's connect your experiences to the course

Activity 1

Tourism Tech Icebreaker

Instructions: Think about your LAST travel experience (or any time you used a tourism service). List ALL the technologies you encountered.

⏱️ Time: 5 minutes individual brainstorm → 5 minutes group sharing
📝 Format: Write on paper or phone notes
💡 Examples: Booked via Agoda? Used Google Maps? Paid with GCash? Checked reviews on TripAdvisor? Received email confirmation?

Discussion

What did you discover?

  • How many technologies did you list? (Most people get 5-10+)
  • Which stage of the value chain had the most technology?
  • Were there moments where technology FAILED or was missing?
  • Did any technology make your experience significantly BETTER?
