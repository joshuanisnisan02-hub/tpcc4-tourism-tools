Deep Dive

Online booking platforms

Let's look at how OTAs actually work from the business side:

  1. Business creates a listing (photos, description, pricing, availability)
  2. OTA displays the listing to millions of potential customers
  3. Customer searches, compares, and books
  4. OTA processes payment and sends confirmation
  5. Business receives booking details and prepares for guest
  6. After service, OTA collects commission (15-25%)
  7. Customer leaves a review on the platform

Deep Dive

Digital payments in PH tourism

Most Used

  • GCash – dominant mobile wallet
  • Maya (PayMaya) – second largest
  • Bank transfer – BDO, BPI, UnionBank
  • Credit/Debit cards – for bigger transactions
  • Cash – still significant in rural areas

Impact on Tourism

  • Enables advance deposits (fewer no-shows)
  • Instant confirmation of payment
  • No need to carry large cash amounts
  • Digital receipt for record-keeping
  • Enables online/remote bookings
Deep Dive

Review platforms & analytics

  • TripAdvisor: #1 travel review site globally, influences booking decisions
  • Google Reviews: Shows in search results, huge local impact
  • OTA reviews (Agoda, Booking.com): Directly affects listing ranking
  • Social media comments: Public feedback, viral potential
  • Why it matters: 93% of travelers say online reviews influence their decisions

⚠️ One viral negative review can cost thousands in lost bookings. Managing online reputation is NOW a core tourism skill.

Smart Tourism

What is Smart Tourism?

"Smart tourism uses information and communication technologies to enhance the tourist experience, improve destination management, and create value for all stakeholders."

Smart Destinations

WiFi zones, info kiosks, sensor-based crowd management

Smart Experiences

Personalized recommendations, location-based services

Smart Business

Data ecosystems, collaborative platforms, shared analytics

Practical Takeaway

What this means for you

  • Learn the basics well: Google Workspace, Canva, social media, GCash – master these first
  • Understand systems: Know WHAT a PMS, CRM, OTA does even if you don't operate one yet
  • Stay adaptable: New tools appear yearly; the concepts stay the same
  • Think function, not brand: "How do I solve this problem?" not "What software is this?"
  • Be the bridge: Many businesses need someone who can connect tech to tourism needs
