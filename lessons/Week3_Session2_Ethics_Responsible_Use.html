Part 1

Customer Data
& Consent

Handling personal information with care

Data Types

What customer data do we handle?

📋 Basic Info

Full name, contact number, email, address, nationality, birthday

🛂 Travel Docs

Passport number, visa info, travel insurance, emergency contacts

💳 Financial

Payment details, credit card numbers, billing address, transaction records

🏨 Preferences

Room type, dietary restrictions, allergies, special requests, accessibility needs

📱 Digital

Social media profiles, IP addresses, booking history, communication records

📸 Media

Guest photos, videos, testimonials, review content

Consent

The principle of consent

"Only collect data you NEED, only use it for the PURPOSE stated, only keep it as long as NECESSARY, and only share it with people who have LEGITIMATE access."

  • Collect: Only what's needed for the service (dili extra "just in case")
  • Inform: Tell customers WHY you're collecting and HOW you'll use it
  • Use: Only for stated purpose (booking info ≠ marketing spam)
  • Protect: Store securely, limit access, use strong passwords
  • Delete: Remove when no longer needed

Part 2

Copyright &
Content Ethics

Using photos, text, and designs responsibly

Copyright Basics

What you can't freely use

  • Other people's photos from Google Images, Instagram, or websites without permission
  • Competitor's marketing materials – copying their poster design or text
  • Stock photos without license – free sites have rules too
  • Music in videos – copyrighted songs without license
  • Text/content – copying blog posts, descriptions, or reviews from others

⚠️ "Found on Google" does NOT mean "free to use." Every image has an owner.

Solutions

Where to get legal content

📸 Take your own

Best option! Original photos are authentic ug exclusively yours

🎁 Free stock sites

Unsplash, Pexels, Pixabay – free with attribution or license

🎨 Create in Canva

Canva's library is licensed for commercial use within the tool

🤝 Ask permission

DM the photographer: "Can we use your photo with credit?"

👥 Guest content

Ask guests: "Can we repost your photo?" (with credit)

📜 Creative Commons

Search for CC-licensed content; follow the specific license terms

Best Practices

Ethical marketing principles

  • Show real photos – authentic, current, representative of actual experience
  • Be transparent on pricing – all-inclusive or clearly state what's extra
  • Accurate descriptions – don't oversell; let the experience speak
  • Credit sources – tag photographers, credit guest photos
  • Honest reviews – encourage real feedback; respond professionally to negatives
  • Update information – remove outdated promos, correct errors quickly
Part 4

Scenario
Discussion

Analyze real ethical dilemmas in tourism

Scenario 1

The wrong price

🚨 Scenario

A tour operator accidentally posted a promo on Facebook: "Bohol 3D2N – ₱999 only!" (meant to type ₱9,999). The post got 500 shares overnight. 47 people messaged wanting to book at ₱999.

Discussion questions:

  • Is the business obligated to honor the wrong price?
  • What's the most professional way to handle this?
  • How could this have been prevented?
Scenario 2

The unauthorized photo

🚨 Scenario

A resort staff member took a photo of a celebrity guest at the pool without their knowledge and posted it on the resort's Facebook page: "Look who's staying with us! 🌟" The post went viral but the guest was furious.

Discussion questions:

  • What privacy right was violated?
  • Even if not a celebrity – would this be okay for any guest?
  • What's the proper way to feature guests in your marketing?
Scenario 3

The copied poster

🚨 Scenario

A new tour operator saw a competitor's beautiful Canva poster. They downloaded it, changed the business name and contact number, and posted it as their own promo material. The competitor noticed and publicly called them out.

Discussion questions:

  • Is changing the name enough to make it "your own"?
  • What are the consequences of being publicly called out?
  • What should the new operator have done instead?
Scenario 4

The mishandled customer info

🚨 Scenario

A travel agency assistant was organizing bookings and accidentally sent a group email with ALL customer names, emails, and booking details visible to everyone in CC (not BCC). 35 customers saw each other's personal information.

Discussion questions:

  • What data protection principle was violated?
  • What should the business do immediately?
  • How can this be prevented in the future?
Summary

Ethical digital checklist

Customer Data ✅

  • Collect only what's needed
  • Inform + get consent
  • Store securely (passwords, limited access)
  • Never share without permission
  • Delete when no longer needed

Content & Marketing ✅

  • Use own photos or licensed content
  • Credit all sources
  • Be honest in pricing and descriptions
  • Don't copy competitor materials
  • Update/remove outdated info
